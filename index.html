About Me

Supply chain
by training. Cross-border
by instinct.

I grew up between Texas and Mexico, studied Supply Chain Management at UT Austin, and then took what was supposed to be a short stint in Shanghai — which turned into one of the better decisions I've made.

I spent two years building commercial bridges between China and Latin America — prospecting from Shanghai, opening accounts with major Mexican retailers, and learning how goods actually move across the planet. In parallel I was doing procurement consulting for a Mexico City firm, sourcing manufacturers in India and generating real cost reductions. Not glamorous. Genuinely useful.

Now I'm back in Mexico City, working in Boom Bite and looking for a full-time role with a global team where cross-border experience is an asset, not a footnote.
